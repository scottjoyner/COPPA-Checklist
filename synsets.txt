line 3 contents > Where personal information has been defined as any of the following

line 4 contents - [ ] full name;

line 5 contents - [ ] home or other physical address, including street name and city or town;

line 6 contents - [ ] online contact information like an email address or other identifier that permits someone to contact a person directly — for example, an IM identifier, VoIP identifier, or video chat identifier;

line 7 contents - [ ] screen name or user name where it functions as online contact information;

line 8 contents - [ ] telephone number;

line 9 contents - [ ] Social Security number;

line 10 contents - [ ] a persistent identifier that can be used to recognize a user over time and across different sites, including a cookie number, an IP address, a processor or device serial number, or a unique device identifier;

line 11 contents - [ ] a photo, video, or audio file containing a child’s image or voice;

line 12 contents - [ ] geolocation information sufficient to identify a street name and city or town; or

line 13 contents - [ ] other information about the child or parent that is collected from the child and is combined with one of these identifiers.

line 15 contents - [ ] request, prompt, or encourage the submission of information, even if it’s optional;

line 16 contents - [ ] let information be made publicly available (for example, with an open chat or posting function) unless you take reasonable measures to delete all or virtually all personal information before postings are public and delete all information from your records; or

line 17 contents - [ ] passively track a child online.

line 18 contents ### Step 2: Post a Privacy Policy that Complies with COPPA.

line 19 contents > To comply with COPPA, your privacy policy should be clear and easy to read. It must contain the following

line 20 contents - [ ] A list of all operators collecting personal information.

line 21 contents   - [ ] the types of personal information collected from children (for example, name, address, email address, hobbies, etc.);

line 22 contents   - [ ] how the personal information is collected — directly from the child or passively, say, through cookies;

line 23 contents   - [ ] how the personal information will be used (for example, for marketing to the child, notifying contest winners, or allowing the child to make information publicly available through a chat room); and

line 24 contents   - [ ] whether you disclose personal information collected from kids to third parties. If you do, your privacy policy must list the types of businesses you disclose information to (for example, ad networks) and how they use the information.

line 25 contents - [ ] A description of the personal information collected and how it’s used.

line 26 contents - [ ] A description of parental rights.

line 27 contents   - [ ] that you won’t require a child to disclose more information than is reasonably necessary to participate in an activity;

line 28 contents   - [ ] that they can review their child’s personal information, direct you to delete it, and refuse to allow any further collection or use of the child’s information;

line 29 contents   - [ ] that they can agree to the collection and use of their child’s information, but still not allow disclosure to third parties unless that’s part of the service (for example, social networking); and

line 30 contents   - [ ] the procedures to follow to exercise their rights.
